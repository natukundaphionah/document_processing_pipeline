# Sunbird AI
## About Sunbird AI
Sunbird AI is a non-profit organization that develops artificial intelligence systems for social impact in Africa.
Led by a team with extensive experience of AI systems in Africa, we aim to help social initiatives, public institutions and policymakers develop new AI technology for their own needs.
We are physically located in Kampala, Uganda.
Sunbird AI is a non-profit organisation registered in Uganda with a 501(c)(3) Equivalence Determination status. Sunbird AI was registered in 2019 with the goal of developing practical AI systems for social good and in turn generating evidence for decision making and policy through these systems.
In particular, we aim for leadership by example: we develop open systems that can be replicated or expanded by others. For this to happen, we are committed to open source and open research, so that others can build on what we are doing.
Sunbird AI has a practical focus, and systems development work is in response to a specific need from some partner organisation. Our projects start life in discussion with local partner organisations, where we assess if technology can be used practically, ethically and usefully to solve a problem. Not all of our projects involve building systems for end users, though.
We are also interested in creating the resources (e.g. software or datasets) which enable others to create practical AI systems. We also aim to be a neutral source of advice for African institutions on the practical possibilities, benefits and risks of AI technology.
For example, the ethics of artificial intelligence are a complex and rapidly evolving field, where it is necessary to evaluate biases, privacy concerns and factors in data technology: as well as maintaining ethical standards on our own projects, we wish to help other organisations to do so. We are also committed to building African technical capacity, in order to have a team who understand both the social context and also the technological opportunities.
Sunbird AI is currently led by Dr. Ernest Mwebaze, who serves as the Executive Director (CEO-equivalent leadership role). (Sunbird AI)

## Projects of Sunbird AI
### 1. Green Electrification Planning (Mini-Grid Site Identification)
**ABOUT THIS PROJECT**
Uganda is endowed with great renewable energy potential, able to generate power from solar, hydro, biomass and wind. However, only about 27% of the country’s population has access to any kind of electricity. Outside of the cities, the number drops to just 10%. This low electrification rate is considered a major barrier for economic development and poverty reduction.
Sunbird AI is supporting the German Agency for International Cooperation (GIZ) and The Ministry of Energy and Mineral Development in implementing the National Electrification Strategy (NES) which provides a strategy aimed at connecting 10 million households to the most optimal energy source by 2030. We are developing an AI based open data site identification system to increase the accuracy of planning assumptions of selections for renewable energy sites and energy source options in Lamwo district.
Visit our online tool for electrification of Lamwo district to view options for electrification of the district.
**MACHINE LEARNING FOR ELECTRIFICATION**
We use machine learning to automate the determination of optimal electrification strategies. To do this we use several remote sensing datasets including the open buildings dataset, population data, NDVI, and several Uganda Ministry of Energy and Mineral Development datasets.
We aim to go beyond understanding the current needs for power to making forecasts for the years ahead. To do this, we are taking information about changes in buildings over time and training models to forecast the expansion or reduction of different settlements.

## 3. Translation &amp; Language Technology
### Sunflower Multilingual Assistant
A multilingual assistant that provides accurate translations, explanations, summaries and other cross-lingual tasks in 31 Ugandan languages.
- Translates across 31 languages
- Provides summaries and explanations in local languages
- Conversational capabilities in Ugandan languages
Sunflower by Sunbird AI is a powerful language technology platform that first focused on supporting Ugandan languages, an important achievement in making digital tools accessible to local communities. By prioritizing Uganda's languages, the platform helps preserve culture, promote inclusion, and ensure that people can interact with technology in the languages they use every day.
Sunflower already provides a wide range of capabilities in Ugandan languages, including text-to-text translation, which allows written content to be translated between English and local languages as well as between supported languages. It also supports text-to-speech, where written text is converted into natural spoken audio, helping users who prefer listening or have reading difficulties. In addition, the platform offers speech-to-text, which converts spoken language into written text for easier communication, transcription, and digital interaction.
Beyond these core language features, Sunflower also supports explanations, summarization, and general question answering. Users can ask questions, receive simplified explanations, and generate summaries of long content in their preferred language, making the platform useful in education, healthcare, agriculture, government services, and business communication.
Having successfully built support for Ugandan languages, Sunflower is now expanding to African languages, bringing the same innovative tools to a wider audience across the continent. Through this growth, Sunbird AI is creating technology that starts in Uganda and scales across Africa.
## 4. Environmental Sensing / Noise Monitoring
They have built sensors to monitor noise pollution in cities (Kampala, Entebbe) to aid urban planning and public health. Africa is rapidly urbanising, and this comes with new public health
hazards. Some problems are easily overlooked because of a lack of data. Noise pollution, for example, may seem merely an annoyance, but in fact is a major health concern. It is linked to deaths from ischaemic heart disease (as the body's nervous system is constantly 'on alert' in noisy places), causes learning impairment in children who cannot sleep well, and poor recovery of patients recovering from illness who need to rest. In Europe, noise pollution is linked to one million healthy years of life lost each year. In Africa, we do not yet know the scale of the problem.
Sunbird AI is working with Entebbe City Authority and KCCA to help understand the acoustic landscape of the city. We are developing custom embedded hardware and installing it around the city to collect data on the sources of noise and sound pressure levels. This will make available, for the first time, comprehensive, real-time data on noise, which can be used for planning and mitigation.
Visit noise.sunbird.ai to view the real-time noise levels in Kampala and Entebbe

## ABOUT THE PROJECT
## SEMA Uganda
We help SEMA Uganda to deploy feedback devices outside hospital wards and police stations (pictured above). Previously, these devices only captured "happy" or "unhappy" emojis. Now, citizens can conveniently record voice feedback in their preferred Ugandan language and articulate their issues: for example, to report that the drugs they needed were out of stock.
Sunbird AI has developed the hardware and the language models which make it possible to transcribe, translate and anonymously summarise people's spoken feedback. This information is relayed to the responsible authorities by SEMA.
## TRAC FM
In Uganda, radio talk shows provide a crucial platform for citizens to voice their opinions, with millions tuning in weekly. TRAC FM, our partner, attracts 20,000 responses, via both text messages and phone calls, weekly and reaches millions of listeners. However, the manual processing of thousands of text messages and phone calls is resource-intensive.
## Uganda Ministry of ICT and National Guidance
Sunbird AI is proud to partner with the Ministry of ICT and National Guidance in Uganda to support the Parish Development Model (PDM) initiative. The PDM aims to uplift rural communities by providing economic opportunities. However, language barriers, with many beneficiaries unable to speak English, hinder effective communication.
To address this challenge, Sunbird AI is providing advanced machine translation technology. By integrating our tools into the PDM information system, we facilitate communication between government officials and citizens. This ensures that information is accessible to all, and that feedback can be effectively gathered and understood.
## Sunbird AI's Ethical Commitment
Sunbird AI operates with a strong commitment to ethical principles. We emphasize transparency, data privacy, and the importance of developing AI models that are tailored to Africa's diverse cultural and social context.
## Sunbird AI on Data Privacy and Bias
- Sunbird AI addresses data privacy by avoiding the collection of personally identifiable information whenever possible.
- When data is necessary, it is handled with strict protocols to ensure privacy and security.
- To address bias, they carefully curate datasets, test models for fairness across different demographics, and ensure AI systems are representative and culturally sensitive to the communities they serve.
Sunbird is dedicated to open-source technology and open research, which allows other organizations and researchers to build upon their work and create their own practical AI systems.
We serve as a neutral source of advice for African institutions on the possibilities, benefits, and risks of AI technology.

## Building Technical Skills at Sunbird AI
Sunbird AI contributes to building technical skills by creating and providing hands-on opportunities for aspiring AI professionals. This includes running its Fellows and Internship Programs, which equip students and researchers with industry experience in machine learning and computer science.
The organization’s commitment to open-source technology and open research means it publishes its datasets and models. This allows other individuals and research teams to learn from and build upon their work, significantly accelerating skill development and innovation across the continent.

## Sunbird AI and Open-Source Technology
Open-source technology is a cornerstone of Sunbird AI's capacity-building efforts. By making its datasets, code, and models publicly available, the organization allows other researchers, developers, and grassroots projects to build on its foundation.
For instance, the Sunbird African Language Technology (SALT) dataset is a freely available, open-source resource. This strategy democratizes access to the tools and data needed to create AI systems for local languages, empowering a new generation of AI professionals and researchers to innovate without needing to start from scratch.
This approach is a deliberate effort to build a sustainable, self-sufficient AI ecosystem in Africa.
## Long-Term Goals of Sunbird AI
The long-term goals for the impact of Sunbird AI's work focus on creating sustainable change through evidence-informed policy. The organization aims to:
- Build a portfolio of successful cases that demonstrate how AI can serve as a powerful tool for social good.
- Develop open and replicable systems that other organizations can use to scale impact.
- Cultivate a sustainable AI ecosystem in Africa by training and empowering a critical mass of skilled local professionals who can design and implement AI solutions tailored to the continent's specific needs and cultural contexts.
The ultimate goal is to create a future where AI technology is widely used to inform and guide policy decisions that improve the well-being of African communities.

## ABOUT THE INTERNSHIP PROGRAM
The Sunbird AI internship program is a 3 months program where students at university or research institutes can spend 3 months working with Sunbird AI on projects of interest to both parties. The fellowship is mainly targeted at equipping the candidate with industry experience in the field of machine learning and computer science.
The program provides an opportunity to the intern to work on a real world practical AI project that is potentially impactful to the societal context of Sunbird AI. The internship can be conducted part-time or full-time, online or physically at the Sunbird AI offices depending on the nature of the project.
The student internship program is an unpaid internship where the candidate benefits from interfacing with leading AI professionals and software and hardware developers working on exciting for-social-good projects.
The candidate is expected to have basic knowledge of computer programming in Python language and a minimum understanding of machine learning. An illustrative GitHub account is a significant advantage to getting this internship.
Internship applicants must be 2nd and 3rd year students.
