# ÈkÓ 5A - EVERYDAY'S GREETINGS AND SALUTATION

As mentioned previously, greeting each other is very important and is the norm throughout the day in Yorùbá culture. There is almost a greeting word for almost anything and every situation. Below are some every day greeting and salutation words.

## NOW LET'S PRACTICE

|   | YORUBA GREETINGS (Verbal / spoken and version) | Meaning in English / EQUIVALENT  |
| --- | --- | --- |
|  1. | Ę ká àárò | Good morning  |
|  2. | Ę ká àsán | Good afternoon  |
|  3. | Ę kù ròlè | Good evening (early evening)  |
|  4. | Ę ká alé | Good evening (late evening)  |
|  5. | Ę kú ìjókòó | Literarily means “sitting well” or hope you are ok” / you all right? This is just to acknowledge the person that is waiting to see someone, a visitor.  |
|  6. | Ę kú ùkàlè | As above.  |
|  7. | Ę kú ilé / Ę kú ulé | Hello I’m back.  |
|  8. | Ę ká àbò | Welcome / welcome back  |
|  9. | Ę kú ìsè / Ę kú usè | Well done (applauding / appreciating that one is working hard)  |
|  10. | Ó dàárò | Good night  |
|  11. | Ó dìgbà | Goodbye  |
|  12. | Ępèlè | This is an empathy word. It means something like ‘sorry! Or I sympathise with you for being in pain (something like that etc.) This is used when someone is in pain (like hitting their body against something), sick and in pain, falls, trips, there is a mishap and so on.  |
|  13. | À á máa ríra | We’ll see later  |
|  14. | Mà ári è | I’ll see you later  |
|  15. | Báwo ni | Hello / how is it?  |
|  16. | Ę kú ìnáwó | Thanks for your gift, thanks for your spending, thanks for inviting us to your party etc  |
