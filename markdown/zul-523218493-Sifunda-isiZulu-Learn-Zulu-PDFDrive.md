## Cha angikwazi - No

There are two ways to form a negative statement in Zulu: one is with **a**- the other is with **-nga-**. The **a**- form is used to make a negative statement eg he doesn't smoke.

Most negatives in the present tense begin with an extra **a**- and change the final vowel to **-i** eg:

- Angikwazi I don't know
- Angikhumbuli I can't remember
- Angethembi kanjalo I don't believe so
- Asifuni amanzi we don't want water
- Abathandi le ndawo they don't like this place
- Awuhambi manje mnumzane? aren't you going now sir?
- Angifundi ukukhlala I don't want to complain
- Amanzi awabandi kakhulu the water isn't very cold
- Igane ayifuni ukudla the child doesn't want to eat
- Anibhemi? you don't smoke?

The third person present negative for class 1, 1a nouns - replace initial **u**- with **aka**- eg akakwazi he doesn't know, umama akayini the lady she isn't ironing and **uMatthew akayi** Matthew isn't going

Passive verbs do not change the end vowel eg anganelisiwe I'm not satisfied, angizwa kahle I don't understand = hear well and akubhenywa lapha no smoking allowed here

# Lesson 9

## Ushukela nobisi - And

And is show by the formative na- which replaces the initial vowel

For nouns beginning with u- the na- becomes no- eg:

- Isithelo nohlaza Fruit and vegetables
- Omama nobaba Mother and father

For nouns beginning with i- the na- becomes ne- eg:

- Uhlaza nesithelo Vegetables and fruit
- IsiZulu nesiSuthu Zulu and Suthu

For nouns beginning with a- the na- is unchanged eg:

- Abafana namantombazane Boys and girls
- Amadoda nabafazi Men and women

## Awugcwalise - Please

Friendly instructions use the formative awu- or maka-

ukugcwalisa to make full, fill up, fill in (a form)

ukusizo tohelp

ukusula to wipe off

ukuvala to close

ukucela to ask for

A friendly way to ask is to attach awu- (or mawu-) to the front of the verb and end the verb with an -e eg:

- Awugcwalise Please fill up
- Awusule Please wipe off
- Awusilethele imeneiyu Please bring us (= -si-) the menu
- Awuvale umnyango mnumzane Please close the door sir
- Awungisize lapha baba Please lend a hand here father

When the request is directed at the 3rd person singular (class 1, 1a) use maka- eg:

- Makalinde uThaniwe Thandiwe must wait please
- Makasale uDavid Please stay David
- Makashayele baba Please drive dad

Polite requests are also made using ukucela (to request)

You use ukucela to ask for something eg ngicela usizo will you help please? = I request your help, or sicela ujama can we have some jam?

Ngicela ... is a generally polite way to ask for something eg ngicela ukubona umama wami I ask to see my mother, umfowetu, ngicela ukumbona my brother, I ask to see him

# Lesson 19

## Ngingajabula - Reciprocal Verbs

A verb stem may be used to mean a reciprocal relationship using the suffix -an- before the final vowel

- bonana see each other
- shayana beat each other
- vimbana stop each other

This is used in two ways - (a) in the plural eg:

- Siyabonana We see each other
- Abatwana bayathandana The children like each other

(b) for multiple subjects using na- (and):

- Abatwana nabafazi bayathandana The children and the women like each other
- Izinkomo nezingwe kazithandani Cattle and leopards don't like each other

Reciprocal verbs do not take an object but do take an adverb:

- Babonana kaningi They see each other often

If both parties implied in the subject are mentioned one is named using its subject concord and the other using na- after the verb:

- Abatwana babonana nabafazi The children and the women see each other = the children seen one and other with the women
- Izinkomo kazithandani nebhubesi The cattle and the lion don't like each other

# Lesson 20

## Causative verbs

### Simsebenzisa umsebenzi wakhe

Verb stems are used to express cause, help, let do using the extension -is- or using the alternative endings -sa for -ka and -za for -la

- hambisa cause to walk
- enzisa help do
- susa take away -suka go away
- veza cause to appear -vela appear

NB: alternative forms:

- -fudumalisa and -fudumeza warm -fudumala be warm
- -ambathisa and -ambesa clothe, cover -ambatha dress

Causative verbs may take two objects:

- Wakhisa ubaba indlu He helps father build a house
- Simsebenzisa umsebenzi wakhe We cause him to do his work

One subject belongs to the basic verb stem eg -sebenza and the other to the causative stem eg -sebenzisa. The first is expressed using the noun eg umsebenzi - the second is expressed using the object concord eg m- and/or the noun eg indlu. The object of the basic verb comes last.

Causative verbs mean 'just like' using the the relative concord oku- with a possesive:

- Uhijimisa okukanogwaja He runs like a hare

- Lesi-silwane silumisa okwnja His wild animal bites like a dog

Passive formed like other stems using -w- - NB: palatization always happens for m, b, ph, mb:

- -boniswa cause to see
- -gijinyiswa cause to run
- Le-nyanda sayiboshiswa ngumama We were helped by mother to tie up this bundle

The applied verb form is used to mean by oneself, alone using the reflexive formative -zi-:

- Ukuzihlalela To sit alone

- Ngizikuzihambelangoba kawufuni ukuhamba nami I will walk alone because you don't want to walk with me

- Indlu yokudlela A house to eat in
- Yincwadi yokulobela lena This is a book in which to write

A locative after a applied verb means the action is applied to a place or direction:

- Ukubuyela ekhaya To return home
- Umfana wawela emgodini A boy fell in a hole

The passive applied stem is sometimes used idiomatically:

- Umfazi wafelwa ngabantwana bakhe The women's children died for her = her children died
- Washonelwa yilanga esendleleni The sun set for her along the road = while she was on the road dusk fell

With -ni? applied verbs express what for or why:

- Uhamelani? For what = why are you going?
- Bangifunelani? Why do they look for me?

NB: passives formed in the usual way, with patatization.

## Consonants

kh much like can - ikhanda head
k - the 'soft-k' is somewhere between k and g - ukuvuka wake up

ng as in finger - ingane child
ny like vignette -inyoni bird

(m)b sounds like bat - imbuzi goat

h is said as in hand - hamba go
